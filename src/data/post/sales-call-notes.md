## The Sales Call Documentation Problem

The average sales rep spends 21% of their day on administrative tasks—writing call notes, updating CRM fields, composing follow-up emails. That's roughly 100 minutes in an 8-hour day not spent selling.

After a call, the typical process is: open CRM, remember what was discussed, type notes from memory, add next steps, send a follow-up email. By the time you finish, the call details are already fading.

AI sales call notes solve this at the root. Instead of documenting from memory after the call, the AI captures everything during the call and delivers a structured summary the moment the conversation ends.

---

## What AI Captures from Sales Calls

A good AI sales call tool extracts:

**Prospect information:** Company, role, use case, current pain points, budget signals, decision-making process discussed.

**Objections raised:** Every objection the prospect raised during the call, extracted and categorized.

**Next steps committed:** What was agreed for next steps—by the rep and by the prospect.

**Timeline and urgency signals:** Any mention of timelines, deadlines, or urgency language that indicates buying intent.

**Stakeholders mentioned:** Names and roles of other people involved in the decision.

**Summary for CRM:** A concise paragraph suitable for pasting into the CRM deal notes field.

## Privacy Matters on Sales Calls

This is important: your prospects didn't consent to their words being sent to Fireflies' or Otter's servers. When you use cloud-based call recorders, the prospect's side of the conversation—their company information, their concerns, their budget discussions—is transmitted to a third party.

Some prospects will notice the bot participant. Many will ask what it is. A few will be uncomfortable with it.

Siplinx AI captures your call audio locally on your device. No bot joins the call. The prospect doesn't see an extra participant. And their words stay on your machine—not on a vendor's server.

For enterprise sales, financial services, healthcare, and legal sectors—where prospects are particularly sensitive about data handling—local processing is the right choice.

## Setting Up Your AI Sales Call Workflow

**Step 1: Capture**
Open Siplinx AI before the call starts. It detects call audio automatically and begins transcribing.

**Step 2: Review**
After the call, review the generated summary. It takes 2–3 minutes to read and correct any errors.

**Step 3: CRM update**
Copy the deal summary into your CRM. Add the extracted next steps as tasks. Done.

**Step 4: Follow-up email**
Use the action items and key discussion points to write a follow-up email. Most of it is already structured—you're editing, not writing from scratch.

Total post-call time: 5–10 minutes instead of 20–30.

## What to Look for in a Sales Call AI Tool

**Accuracy for business vocabulary.** Product names, industry terms, and prospect company names should transcribe correctly. Test with your specific vocabulary before committing.

**Speed of delivery.** You want notes available within seconds of the call ending, not 10 minutes later.

**Privacy posture.** If your prospects would be uncomfortable knowing their words were sent to a third party, choose a local processing tool.

**CRM-ready output.** The summary should be structured for easy CRM entry, not just a raw transcript dump.
