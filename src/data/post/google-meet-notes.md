## Google Meet's Native Transcription: What It Can and Can't Do

Google Workspace subscribers with Business Standard plans or above can enable Meet's built-in transcription. It auto-generates a `.docx` transcript in Google Drive after the meeting. For basic needs, this works.

**Limitations:**

- Requires Google Workspace Business plan—personal Gmail users can't access it
- Produces raw transcripts only—no AI summary, no action items
- Stores data in Google Drive (which has its own privacy implications for sensitive discussions)
- Accuracy is adequate but not best-in-class for technical vocabulary or accents
- No speaker diarization improvement—labels may be inaccurate

For teams that need structured summaries, action item extraction, or anything beyond a raw text dump, third-party tools do significantly better.

## Privacy Considerations for Google Meet Notes

When a third-party bot joins your Google Meet call, two things happen: the bot participant is visible to all attendees, and the audio is streamed to that company's servers. This matters because:

- External guests see the bot in the participant list and may ask about it
- Your audio—including everything your guests say—is processed by a third party
- Retention, deletion, and training data policies vary by vendor

With Siplinx AI's local processing, none of these issues arise. The app captures audio at the OS level, invisible to other participants, and all processing stays on your machine.
