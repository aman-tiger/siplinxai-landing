## Why Teams Look for a Fireflies Alternative

Fireflies.ai dominates the AI meeting recorder space through aggressive marketing and a generous free tier. For many users, it's the first AI notetaker they try. But frequent complaints in reviews reveal three recurring problems:

**Bot visibility.** Fireflies joins your call as a named bot participant. In sales calls, this can make prospects uncomfortable. In sensitive negotiations, it can undermine trust. In interviews or therapy-adjacent sessions, it creates an obvious third presence.

**Cloud processing.** Fireflies processes all audio on remote servers. Every word spoken by every participant—including external guests, clients, and partners—is transmitted to Fireflies' infrastructure. Users have no practical way to prevent this after they've enabled the integration.

**Cost at scale.** Fireflies' free tier is limited to short meetings with restricted AI features. Business plans scale quickly, and the per-seat pricing adds up fast for larger teams.

### Fathom — Best Free Alternative

Fathom offers a genuinely unlimited free tier for individual users on Zoom. No meeting caps, no minutes limits. It joins calls silently (no visible bot on most platforms), produces clean summaries, and is genuinely fast. The catch: it's cloud-based and primarily Zoom-focused. Team features cost extra.

**Best for:** Individual users on Zoom who don't work with sensitive data.

### Otter.ai — Best for Captions

Otter.ai offers strong real-time captions and a well-known brand. Its free tier includes 300 minutes/month. The product is cloud-based and has gone through many UX pivots, but the core transcription is reliable for general use.

**Best for:** Users who need live captions for accessibility or occasional transcription at low volume.

### tl;dv — Best for Async Teams

tl;dv focuses on creating shareable video clips and timestamped summaries. If your team reviews meetings async and values the ability to share specific moments, tl;dv has better clip tools than most. Still cloud-based.

**Best for:** Remote-first teams focused on async video review.
