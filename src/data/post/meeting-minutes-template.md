## Meeting Notes vs. Meeting Minutes: What's the Difference?

The terms are often used interchangeably, but they mean different things in professional contexts.

**Meeting notes** are informal records of what was discussed. They're personal, internal, and don't need to follow any standard format. Most workplace note-taking falls into this category.

**Meeting minutes** are formal records of what was _decided_ in an official meeting. They're often required for board meetings, committee sessions, legal proceedings, and corporate governance contexts. Minutes are typically:

- Dated and signed off by a secretary or chair
- Structured around motions, votes, and resolutions
- Used as legal documentation that what was decided is on record

For most workplace meetings, you want structured **meeting notes** that include decisions, action items, and key discussion points—not necessarily formal parliamentary minutes. AI tools produce this automatically.

## What Good Meeting Minutes Include

Whether you're writing formal minutes or structured work meeting notes, these elements should always appear:

**Header:**

- Meeting title and purpose
- Date, time, and location (or platform)
- Attendees present (and absent if relevant)
- Who facilitated and who took notes

**Body:**

- Agenda items covered (in order)
- Key discussion points per agenda item
- Decisions made (explicitly marked)
- Votes taken and results (for formal meetings)

**Footer:**

- Action items with assigned owners and due dates
- Next meeting date and agenda preview (if set)
- Signature of minute-taker (for formal minutes)

## Meeting Minutes Template

```

Date: [Date]
Meeting: [Name/Purpose]
Location: [Room / Platform]
Attendees: [Names]
Facilitator: [Name]

AGENDA & DISCUSSION

1. [Agenda Item 1]
   Summary: [Key points discussed]
   Decision: [What was decided]

2. [Agenda Item 2]
   Summary: [Key points discussed]
   Decision: [What was decided]

ACTION ITEMS
- [Action] — Owner: [Name] — Due: [Date]
- [Action] — Owner: [Name] — Due: [Date]

NEXT MEETING
Date: [Date] | Time: [Time]
Agenda preview: [Topics]

Minutes prepared by: [Name]
```

## How AI Generates Meeting Minutes Automatically

Modern AI meeting note tools use a two-step process:

1. **Real-time transcription** — The tool converts speech to text as the meeting happens using ASR (automatic speech recognition) with speaker identification.

2. **LLM post-processing** — After (or during) the meeting, a large language model analyzes the transcript and generates a structured document: agenda items covered, key decisions, action items with owners, and a summary.

The result closely matches what a skilled minute-taker would produce—but with no manual effort, no cognitive split between listening and writing, and no risk of missing critical decisions because you were typing something else.

## Common Mistakes in Meeting Minutes

**Too much detail.** Minutes record decisions, not every word said. Capturing full debate is unnecessary unless parliamentary procedure requires it.

**Missing action items.** The most common failure. Every decision should have a corresponding action item with a named owner.

**No dates.** Action items without due dates get done never. Even an approximate timeframe is better than nothing.

**Writing minutes after the fact from memory.** Real-time AI transcription eliminates this problem entirely—the record exists immediately after the meeting.
