## The Problem with Manual Zoom Notes

Taking notes during a Zoom call is a cognitive split that hurts both activities. You're either present in the conversation—contributing, listening, making decisions—or you're typing. You can't do both well simultaneously.

The result: most Zoom meeting notes are incomplete, written from memory after the call, or simply don't exist. Action items get missed. Decisions get revisited. Context fades within 24 hours.

AI Zoom transcription solves this by doing the documentation work automatically, in real time, while you stay fully present.

---

## How AI Note-Takers Work with Zoom

There are two approaches to capturing Zoom audio for AI processing:

**Bot-based (cloud):** Tools like Fireflies and Otter deploy a bot that joins your Zoom call as a participant. The bot streams audio to cloud servers for processing. The advantage: easy setup, accessible from any device. The disadvantage: a visible third participant in your call, and all audio leaving your machine.

**System audio capture (local or silent):** Tools like Siplinx AI and Granola capture audio directly at the system level—they listen to what's coming through your speakers and microphone without joining the call as a participant. No bot. No visible presence. Nothing transmitted to the cloud (for local tools).

For sensitive calls—client negotiations, legal discussions, medical consultations—system audio capture with local processing is the only approach that guarantees confidentiality.

## Zoom's Built-In AI Notes vs. Third-Party Tools

Zoom has its own AI Companion feature (Zoom AI) that generates meeting summaries. It's convenient but has limitations: it requires a Zoom Business or higher plan, summaries are basic, and—critically—all processing goes through Zoom's AI infrastructure.

Third-party tools generally offer better summary quality, more flexible export options, and (with Siplinx AI) the option for fully local processing.
