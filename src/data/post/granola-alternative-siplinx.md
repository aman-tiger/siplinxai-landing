## Why People Are Looking for a Granola Alternative

Granola launched as one of the first truly clean, elegant AI meeting note-takers for Mac. It works directly in the background without joining your call as a bot, it produces genuinely good summaries, and the interface feels native and fast. For many users, it became the default tool for Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams recordings.

But as usage grew, several friction points emerged:

**1. Cloud dependency.** Granola processes your audio on remote servers. Every meeting you record is transmitted to external infrastructure. For teams handling sensitive conversations—sales calls discussing unreleased products, legal consultations, healthcare discussions, or executive strategy sessions—this is a significant compliance and confidentiality risk.

**2. Mac-only.** Granola runs only on macOS. Teams with even one Windows user are immediately excluded. Many organizations run mixed environments, and a tool that only serves half the team creates workflow asymmetry.

**3. Subscription cost adds up.** At its current pricing tier, Granola's per-seat cost compounds quickly for growing teams. Organizations that need 10, 20, or 50 seats start comparing total cost of ownership carefully.

**4. No offline mode.** Working from a train, a client's office with restricted Wi-Fi, or any environment without reliable internet? Granola simply doesn't work. Your meeting notes depend on a live connection.

None of this makes Granola a bad tool. For individual Mac users at a startup who don't work with sensitive data, it's a legitimate option. But for the growing segment of users who need privacy, cross-platform support, or offline capability, the search for a Granola alternative is completely rational.

## What to Look for in a Granola Alternative

Before comparing specific tools, it helps to define what actually matters. Most AI meeting note-takers have roughly the same headline features—transcription, summaries, action items. The real differences show up in five areas:

### 1. Where Is Your Data Processed?

This is the most important question. Cloud-based tools (Granola, Fireflies.ai, Otter.ai) send your audio to their servers. Local tools (Siplinx AI) run speech recognition and LLM summarization entirely on your device—nothing ever leaves your machine.

If your meetings contain anything that could be subject to NDA, GDPR, HIPAA, attorney-client privilege, or internal confidentiality policies, local processing isn't a feature. It's a requirement.

### 2. Platform Support

Mac-only tools lock out Windows users. Look for tools that run natively on both macOS and Windows so your entire team can use the same workflow.

### 3. Meeting Platform Compatibility

Does it work with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, and in-person meetings? Some tools require a bot to join your call (creating a visible third participant); others work silently in the background by capturing system audio.

### 4. Accuracy and Language Support

Transcription quality varies significantly across accents, technical vocabulary, and background noise. The best tools use Whisper-class speech recognition with speaker diarization (identifying who said what). Bonus: multilingual meetings are increasingly common—language flexibility matters.

### 5. Long-Term Cost

Per-seat SaaS pricing for multiple users grows fast. Some tools offer lifetime licenses or one-time pricing models that make more sense for stable teams.

## The Privacy Question: What Actually Happens to Your Granola Audio?

When you use Granola, your meeting audio is captured locally on your Mac and then sent to cloud infrastructure for processing. Granola's privacy policy describes how this data is handled, but regardless of what any policy says, the fundamental fact is that your audio—including every word spoken by everyone in your meeting—is transmitted to and processed by external servers.

For users in regulated industries, this creates real exposure:

- **Legal professionals** are subject to attorney-client privilege and bar association ethics rules around client confidentiality. Transmitting client communications to a third-party cloud service may create risk.
- **Healthcare teams** are subject to HIPAA. Patient discussions, case reviews, or any meeting involving protected health information (PHI) cannot legally be processed by a non-HIPAA-compliant vendor without proper agreements in place.
- **Financial advisors and banks** are subject to SEC, FINRA, and other financial regulations around communication retention and privacy.
- **Corporate executives** discussing M&A activity, competitive strategy, or unreleased products are subject to insider trading rules and IP protection requirements.

With Siplinx AI, none of this applies. There is no data transmission. There is no server. There is no third party with access to your audio. Every word stays on your device.
