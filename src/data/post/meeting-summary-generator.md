## What Is a Meeting Summary Generator?

A meeting summary generator is software that automatically produces a structured written record of a meeting. The best tools do this in real time (generating summaries as the meeting progresses) or immediately post-meeting (delivering a complete summary within seconds of the call ending).

Unlike a raw transcript—which is just a word-for-word record of what was said—a meeting summary is a condensed, structured document designed to communicate the meeting's outcomes to people who didn't attend, or to serve as a quick reference for those who did.

A good AI-generated summary includes:
- **Meeting overview:** What the meeting was about and who attended
- **Key decisions:** What was agreed, resolved, or concluded
- **Action items:** Who is doing what, by when
- **Open questions:** Unresolved issues that need follow-up
- **Brief narrative:** A short paragraph summarizing the discussion arc

---

## How AI Generates Meeting Summaries

**Step 1 — Transcription:** The meeting audio is converted to text in real time using automatic speech recognition (ASR). Modern tools use Whisper-class models for this.

**Step 2 — Speaker labeling:** Diarization assigns each transcript segment to a speaker (Speaker A, Speaker B, or by name if the tool integrates with your calendar).

**Step 3 — LLM processing:** A large language model reads the full transcript and identifies the structure of the conversation: what topics were discussed, what conclusions were reached, what tasks were assigned.

**Step 4 — Summary generation:** The LLM writes a structured summary document following a template (decisions, action items, key points).

**Step 5 — Delivery:** The summary is displayed immediately after the meeting ends—no waiting, no upload delays.

---

## Writing a Meeting Summary vs. Generating One

Before AI tools, someone on the call had to write the summary afterward. This created three problems:

**It happened late.** The summary was often written hours after the meeting—when memory had already degraded and the writer couldn't remember the nuance of every decision.

**It depended on one person.** The quality of the summary depended entirely on who was taking notes. Some people are excellent at this; most are not.

**It cost attention during the meeting.** The note-taker was partially absent from the conversation while documenting it.

AI summary generation eliminates all three: the summary is always immediate, always consistent, and doesn't cost anyone any presence during the meeting.
